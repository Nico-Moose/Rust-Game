# Project Vision

## Working title
**MOOSE RUST: WASTELAND**

## Genre
Survival PvP browser mini app with Twitch login, base building, loot runs, PvP, raids, tech progression, seasonal wipes, and stream integrations.

## High concept
Players enter a hostile wasteland with almost nothing, gather resources, build and defend a base, loot dangerous monuments, unlock better equipment through scrap-based progression, fight other players, and eventually raid enemy compounds. The design keeps the emotional DNA of Rust—risk, scarcity, loss, escalation, territorial conflict—but compresses it into short sessions and a clearer browser-friendly loop.

## Pillars
1. **Risk creates value** — loot matters because death matters.
2. **Short sessions, real progress** — 5–20 minutes should feel meaningful.
3. **Base ownership matters** — the base is identity, safety, and long-term investment.
4. **PvP has stakes** — carried loot is always on the line outside safety.
5. **Raids are endgame** — expensive, dramatic, and strategically meaningful.
6. **Progression is visible** — every session should move the player forward.
7. **Stream-friendly drama** — map events, raids, wipes, leaderboards, and overlays create strong Twitch moments.

## Target play fantasy
The player starts poor, nervous, and weak. After a few successful runs they get a starter base, primitive weapons, and enough confidence to push monuments. Mid game opens tech, better defenses, stronger weapons, and territorial conflict. Late game turns the player into a feared raider, a fortified defender, or a wealthy trader whose name matters on the server.

## Why this works as a mini app
- Smaller world size
- Faster resource gain
- Faster base setup
- Reduced maintenance friction
- Stream overlays and spectator readability
- Strong wipe-based retention
